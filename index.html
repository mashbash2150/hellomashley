ABOUT
Deciding it was time to upskill after over a decade spent in engineering-adjacent roles, I've been busy immersing myself in the world of software development with my sights set on breaking in once and for all. I'm proud to have logged over 500 hours of rigorous structured learning as a student in General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive, as well countelss hours of self-guided exploration. To say I'm eager to discover what doors these new skills might open is an understatement.
Though I have previous expertise in deeply technical realms including robotics, automation, and collaborative software applications - I've always had a strong passion for design and aesthetics that I've not yet had the privilege of exploring professionally. Through exercising my front-end chops in class, I've found genuine fulfillment in using my right brain in the development process, which I've drawn from in the making of this website and the works featured here. Somewhere within the intersection between art and technology is where I hope to find myself.
What I love above all else is process, the joy of learning, and adding new skills and experiences to my toolbelt. As a builder and dreamer myself, I can't wait to collaborate with teams daring to question, reimagine, and bring to life the world's next great products and experiences.
Thanks for stopping by!

LEISURE
Spawned from a family of artists, it's no surprise that I love to draw. Find me a better pen than the Micron 0.05. Go ahead...I'll wait.
Occasionally I'll pick up a paintbrush, too. Here's a sign I lettered for a restaraunt in LA's Echo Park neighborhood.
Of all of our 5 senses, I think olfaction is by far the most interesting. I've learned a lot about its mysteries through taking classes at the Institute for Art and Olfaction.
I've taken up TIG and MIG welding in the last year, and have The Crucible in Oakland to thank for their awesome exploratory courses in the industrial arts.
3D modeling and printing is a hobby of mine as well. Here's a portafilter holster I designed and printed for my Gaggia Classic setup at home. I've found that coffee-related problems are fun ones to solve.
I'm fascinated by plants, and love to watch things grow. Beyond gardening, Terarrium-building has been a fun way to explore that.
ad infinitum...
While many would consider leisure irrelevant in the scope of one's professional life, I firmly believe that curiosity, and a willingness to introduce oneself to unfamiliar concepts and problems speaks volumes about a person's willingness to think broadly and persevere beyond failure. Sampling the world has taught me numerous lessons, and I intend to continue filling my plate until my brain can no longer store new information.
